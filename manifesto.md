
# NEXT STEP

## Manifesto

### The Problem

Modern democracies face a fundamental difficulty.

The well-being of a society depends heavily on the quality of its governance. However, governance has become increasingly complex. Economics, education, healthcare, justice, defense, energy, technology and international relations are domains that require extensive knowledge and experience.

As a result, most citizens lack the ability to directly evaluate the performance of governments and public institutions.

This creates two problems.

First, government agents may pursue objectives that are not aligned with the interests of society.

Second, government agents are themselves limited human beings and may simply be incompetent.

Democracy assumes that citizens can supervise those who govern them. In practice, however, citizens often lack the knowledge required to perform that supervision.

### Existing Solution

Societies have already developed a partial solution.

Citizens inform themselves through journalists, commentators, analysts, academics, podcasters, writers and other public figures who evaluate political decisions and government performance.

However, democratic culture currently provides little guidance regarding how citizens should choose and evaluate these evaluators.

### Proposed Solution

This project proposes the adoption of two new democratic values:

1. Delegate the evaluation of government agents.
2. Evaluate the delegates.

Citizens should actively seek individuals capable of evaluating government performance.

Citizens should also evaluate those individuals by demanding that they justify their conclusions and confront competing analyses.

The minimum requirement imposed on evaluators should be that they engage with one another and attempt to resolve disagreements through facts and logic.

In this way, democratic supervision becomes a collective process in which competence can emerge, be recognized and be continuously tested.

### The Next Step

The proposal does not require new institutions, constitutional reform or public funding.

It requires the adoption of new democratic values.

The remainder of this document presents the foundations that support this proposal.
