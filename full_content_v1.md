# NEXT STEP

## Manifesto

### The Problem

Modern democracies face a fundamental difficulty.

The well-being of a society depends heavily on the quality of its governance. However, governance has become increasingly complex. Economics, education, healthcare, justice, defense, energy, technology and international relations are domains that require extensive knowledge and experience.

As a result, most citizens lack the ability to directly evaluate the performance of governments and public institutions.

This creates two problems.

First, government agents may pursue objectives that are not aligned with the interests of society.

Second, government agents are themselves limited human beings and may simply be incompetent.

Democracy assumes that citizens can supervise those who govern them. In practice, however, citizens often lack the knowledge required to perform that supervision.

### Existing Solution

Societies have already developed a partial solution.

Citizens inform themselves through journalists, commentators, analysts, academics, podcasters, writers and other public figures who evaluate political decisions and government performance.

However, democratic culture currently provides little guidance regarding how citizens should choose and evaluate these evaluators.

### Proposed Solution

This project proposes the adoption of two new democratic values:

1. Delegate the evaluation of government agents.
2. Evaluate the delegates.

Citizens should actively seek individuals capable of evaluating government performance.

Citizens should also evaluate those individuals by demanding that they justify their conclusions and confront competing analyses.

The minimum requirement imposed on evaluators should be that they engage with one another and attempt to resolve disagreements through facts and logic.

In this way, democratic supervision becomes a collective process in which competence can emerge, be recognized and be continuously tested.

### The Next Step

The proposal does not require new institutions, constitutional reform or public funding.

It requires the adoption of new democratic values.

The remainder of this document presents the foundations that support this proposal.

# Core

## Chapter 1 - Moral

### Postulate 1

When an individual is faced with two or more alternatives, the morally correct choice is the one that maximizes their well-being.

### Definition 1

Maximum individual well-being is the alternative that an individual would prefer if they had complete knowledge of all emotional states experienced throughout the duration of each alternative.

---

## Chapter 2 - Human Nature

### Definition 2

A rule that helps an individual act according to the moral framework established in Postulate 1 is called a moral value.

### Hypothesis 1 - Human Behaviour

When confronted with choices, human beings make decisions using three tools:

* Intuition
* Moral values
* Reason

### Hypothesis 2

In most situations, intuition dominates.

In more important situations, moral values and reason are also employed.

In highly complex situations, where intuition and existing moral values provide insufficient guidance, human beings rely on reason but face significant difficulty in identifying the correct choice.

### Thesis 1

In complex situations where intuition and existing moral values are insufficient, individuals should seek assistance from other individuals, tools and new values.

---

## Chapter 3 - The Role of the State

The state exists to assist individuals in tasks that they cannot successfully accomplish alone in the pursuit of their well-being.

The existence of a state is therefore a natural consequence of morally correct human behaviour.

The role of the state is to assist individuals in maximizing their well-being.

Because the paths to individual well-being frequently conflict, the actions of the state result from decisions made by the individuals who compose it and by the influence of those around them.

---

## Chapter 4 - The Next Step

### Hypothesis

The values currently present in democratic societies are insufficient for good governance.

### New Democratic Values

1. Participate.
2. Delegate the Evaluation of Government Agents.
3. Evaluate the Delegates.

### Evaluating Delegates

The evaluation of a delegate should be based on:

* Understanding the objective of the function being evaluated.
* Requiring explanations connecting decisions to objectives.
* Requiring self-evaluation when necessary.
* Evaluating competence through the decomposition of complex functions into smaller tasks.

### Consensus Requirement

Delegates share a common function: evaluating government agents.

Therefore, delegates should be expected to engage with one another, resolve disagreements where possible and seek consensus.

Positions that cannot survive scrutiny through facts and logic should be discarded.
